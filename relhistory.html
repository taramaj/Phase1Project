A community garden is any space that is gardened and cared for by a group of people. Community gardens have a long history. In the United States, they first popped up in Detroit during the economic recession of the 1890s. These "vacant lot gardens" were sponsored by the city, and they provided unemployed workers with empty plots of land to cultivate, along with any tools, seeds and instructions needed to start a garden.Detroit. Share in the maintenance of the garden, with tasks including keeping up paths, weeding beds and pruning or harvesting plants. Communal gardens exist to serve the specific needs of its community. Benefits include access to fresh food, encourage outside activity, get close to nature, beautify vacant and abandoned lots, improve the environment, revitalize communities, encourage composting, recycling. Education, get closer with nature, respite from uban bustle, bring together community.
